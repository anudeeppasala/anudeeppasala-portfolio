Parkinson's Disease Detection using Machine Learning Techniques

Anudeep Pasala

Department of Computer Science and Engineering
Sathyabama Institute of Science and Technology, Chennai, India

Published 2014 · Springer conference proceedings format

Abstract

Parkinson disease (PD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that affects more than six million people worldwide. Non-specialist physicians often lack a definitive test for PD, especially in early stages where symptoms may be intermittent and poorly defined. This contributes to high misdiagnosis rates (up to 25% among non-specialists) and years of delay before treatment.

This work presents a predictive analytics pipeline that combines K-means clustering and decision trees on patient voice data, together with principal component analysis (PCA) for feature extraction from spiral-drawing inputs and support vector machine (SVM) classification. The UCI Machine Learning Repository Parkinson's disease voice dataset is used for evaluation. The hybrid approach—merging speech analytics with spiral-drawing analysis—aims to support earlier, more accurate detection so that therapeutic care can begin sooner.

Keywords: Parkinson's disease PCA K-means clustering Decision Tree Machine Learning Data Mining SVM

Introduction

Parkinson's disease is a movement disorder that affects the nervous system and worsens over time. Symptoms often begin with a mild tremor in one hand and general weakness, then progress as dopamine levels in the brain decline. Most cases appear at or after age 60; roughly 5–10% of diagnoses occur before age 50 (early-onset PD). Data mining and machine learning can help clinicians find hidden patterns in growing medical datasets and support earlier diagnosis when treatment is most effective.

Proposed system

The proposed model analyzes both speech signals and spiral drawings so clinicians can compare outcomes and recommend care based on disease stage.

  • Voice data processing: Parkinson's disease voice data from the UCI repository is analyzed in RStudio using K-means clustering and decision trees to reduce error and surface patient-level insights.
  • Spiral drawing analysis: Spiral images are processed in Python (PyCharm). PCA extracts features from drawings; grip angle, pressure, timestamp, and reference IDs are removed before SVM classification against a trained database.

A hybrid workflow integrates voice analytics and image-based spiral analysis so healthcare teams can evaluate large, complex datasets more efficiently and pursue earlier intervention.

Experimental results

Experiments used the UCI machine learning repository datasets in R Studio and Python for spiral pressure visualization. The classifier achieved approximately 86.66% accuracy, 99.9% specificity, and 80.48% sensitivity, demonstrating strong potential for assisted early screening when combined with clinical judgment.

Conclusion

Prior work often focused on a single imaging modality or dementia type. This study surveys a broader range of imaging and machine learning approaches for neurodegenerative diagnosis and emphasizes early detection of Parkinson's disease so patients can receive treatment and support as early as possible. Integrating voice and spiral-drawing signals offers a practical path toward more timely, data-driven screening workflows.
